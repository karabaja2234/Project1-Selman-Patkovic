Develop URSELF Association Zenica

Do you want to become a successful freelancer/self-made developer? We are here to guide you. Come and join us!

What is the main goal of DevelopURSELF Association?

It is to motivate people who strive to become developers, throughout multiple courses, different types of forums, made specifically for spreading rare and very useful tips, tricks and benefits within a particular programming language. Of course, there's a variety of tutorials, starting from the ones dedicated to help the community of beginners, but also those which are intended to improve the knowledge of already skilled programmers and web developers. In the next part of the page, you will discover all the possibilities that we have to offer, including many interesting parts, such as developers meet-ups, different competitions, gaming events ect. Stick around and enjoy!

You should definitely visit our main youtube channel, regardless of being a beginner or an advanced developer, programming is an endless stream of knowledge! With over 500k subscribers and more than 150 videos uploaded, we offer daily content including tutorials, free courses, problem-solving videos and much more.

Every 6 months, we organise a new course, taking into a count the difficulty of each of them. The courses which were the most successful in the past 2 years were: Python, JavaScript, HTML & CSS and CSharp. Most importantly, we are giving our best to keep up with the latest programming trends and the languages which are mostly used in the past year.

Be sure to visit our facebook and instagram page, where we tend to organise live streams every week, giving our supporters the chance to ask our main senior-developer team any question regarding our work and programming in general. There is also a live forum, led by one of our admins, where we post free exercises, daily tips and tricks, of course, in arrangement with our followers, to give them what they mostly want.

DevelopURSELF is not only a developing association. It is more social than it appears, because we tend to organise many meet-ups, competitions and gaming events, in order to meet our community and to let them know that they can approach us at any time for help, because that's what this organisation is all about.
